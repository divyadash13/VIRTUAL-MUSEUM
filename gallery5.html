Below are a series of images with detailed descriptions.

Mona Lisa

Artist: Leonardo da Vinci

Location: The Louvre, Paris, France

Description: The Mona Lisa is perhaps the most famous painting in the world. This portrait of a woman with an enigmatic smile has captivated viewers for centuries and is renowned for its detailed execution and mysterious aura.

The Starry Night

Artist: Vincent van Gogh

Location: The Museum of Modern Art (MoMA), New York City, USA

Description: The Starry Night is an iconic oil painting depicting a swirling night sky over a sleepy town. Van Gogh's unique brushwork and vibrant colors have made this painting one of the most recognized in the history of art.

The Persistence of Memory

Artist: Salvador Dalí

Location: The Museum of Modern Art (MoMA), New York City, USA

Description: This surreal painting features melting clocks draped over a barren landscape. It is a quintessential example of Dalí's imaginative and dream-like style, exploring themes of time and reality.

Girl with a Pearl Earring

Artist: Johannes Vermeer

Location: Mauritshuis, The Hague, Netherlands

Description: Often referred to as the "Mona Lisa of the North," this portrait of a young girl wearing an exotic dress and a pearl earring is celebrated for its captivating subject and Vermeer's masterful use of light and shadow.

The Scream

Artist: Edvard Munch

Location: National Gallery, Oslo, Norway

An iconic expressionist painting depicting a figure with an agonized expression against a tumultuous sky. It conveys deep emotion and existential angst.

The Last Supper

Artist: Leonardo da Vinci

Location: Convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie, Milan, Italy

This mural captures the moment Jesus announces his betrayal at the Last Supper. The composition, emotion, and use of perspective showcase da Vinci's genius.
